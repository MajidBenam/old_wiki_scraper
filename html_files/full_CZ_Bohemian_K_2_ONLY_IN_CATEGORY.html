General description[edit]

“The Kingdom of Bohemia… was a medieval and early modern monarchy in Central Europe, the predecessor of the modern Czech Republic. It was an Imperial State in the Holy Roman Empire, and the Bohemian king was a prince-elector of the empire. The kings of Bohemia, besides the region of Bohemia proper itself, also ruled other lands belonging to the Bohemian Crown, which at various times included Moravia, Silesia, Lusatia, and parts of Saxony, Brandenburg, and Bavaria.”[2]

This polity period begins and ends with the rule of the House of Luxembourg. Many kings of this dynasty were also Emperors of Germany. John of Bohemia (John of Luxembourg) was crowned in 1310. His son, Charles IV, went on to become the Holy Roman Emperor as well as the King of Bohemia, ushering in the Golden Age of Bohemia. The period end with the death of King Sigismund in 1437. Bohemia then passed under the rule of the Hapsburg dynasty of Austria-Hungary.

Social Scale[edit]

♠ Polity territory ♣ 1378: 125,000 ♥ in squared kilometers. The territory of Bohemia proper is 52,065 sq km.[4] However the overall total crown lands of Bohemia by the end of Charles IV’s reign (and his territorial gains through each of his four successive marriages) totalled about 125,000 km2. This was Bohemia = 55,000 km2, Moravia = 27,000 km2 and the remaining crown lands of 42,000 km2.[5]

♠ Polity Population ♣ 1350: 2,000,000 ♥ People. It is estimated that there were around 2,000,000 inhabitants in Bohemia in the fourteenth century. [6]The arrival of plague in 1380 led to the deaths of around 15 percent of the population of Bohemia. [7]

♠ Population of the largest settlement ♣ 1400: 40,000 ♥ Inhabitants. Prague was the capital city and largest settlement with around 40,000 inhabitants in 1400.[8]

Law[edit]

♠ Formal legal code ♣ present ♥ Bohemia had its own laws which were codified in the 12th century. Laws were issued directly from the monarch who was believed to have the divine right to legislate.[18]

♠ Judges ♣ present ♥ Judges were placed at provincial and imperial levels. The monarch was the overall judge and lawmaker.[19][20]

♠ Courts ♣ present ♥ There was the royal law court (dvorský soud), which the king presided over, and the land courts (zemský soud) which dealt with all other matters.[21][22]

♠ Professional Lawyers ♣ present ♥ Lawyers were present in Bohemia.[23]
