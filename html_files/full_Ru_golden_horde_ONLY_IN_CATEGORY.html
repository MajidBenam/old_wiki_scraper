Temporal bounds

♠ Duration ♣ 1270-1395 CE ♥

After the death of Temuchin in 1227 CE, the Mongol Empire was divided into different realms. Batu Khan took control of the 'Orda Khan (Blue Horde)' ruling the northwestern part of the Mongol Empire, centred in eastern Europe.

In the 1260s a series of Civil Wars between dependents of Temuchin effectively broke apart the different parts of Mongol Empire. Mengu-Timur took control as Khan of the Golden Horde in 1267, while Kublai Khan consolidated rule over Mongolia and China, which became known as the Great Yuan Dynasty.

In 1395 most of the Horde territory was overrun by the invasions of Timur the Great.

Military Organization[edit]

♠ Standing armies ♣ inferred present ♥ inferred continuity with Mongol Empire period. Chingiz Khan created an imperial guard - keshig. “its nucleus was his original and most faithful followers”. Increased in size to 10, 000 by 1206. [97] Although the Mongols forces may not have been as well armoured equipped as armies who opposed them [or have had as big horses] they had up for this in organisation. All male Mongols under 60 years of age were liable for military service. The Khans could therefore mobilise large numbers of soldiers who were organised into the decimal system. This helped them to deploy their famous formation and tactics on the battlefield. Men provided their own equipment including horses. Originally soldiers paid in booty “divided up according to fixed principles”.[98] Once empire stabilised, officers in China were salaried. In Persia they reverted to the local system of allocating land to soldiers who lived off the produce. [99]
